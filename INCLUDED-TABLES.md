# Included Weather Tables

This module ships with two comprehensive weather roll tables in the **Weather Tables** compendium.

## Standard Weather Patterns (d20)

A realistic weather table for temperate climates. Perfect for standard fantasy campaigns.

| Roll  | Weather       | Temperature | Description                                |
| ----- | ------------- | ----------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| 1-2   | Clear Skies   | 22°C        | Beautiful sunny day with minimal clouds    |
| 3-5   | Partly Cloudy | 20°C        | Pleasant weather with scattered clouds     |
| 6-8   | Overcast      | 18°C        | Gray skies with complete cloud cover       |
| 9-11  | Light Rain    | 16°C        | Gentle rain from gray clouds               |
| 12-14 | Moderate Rain | 15°C        | Steady rainfall, reduced visibility        |
| 15-16 | Heavy Rain    | 14°C        | Intense rainfall with strong winds         |
| 17    | Thunderstorm  | 16°C        | Lightning, thunder, and heavy rain         |
| 18    | Fog           | 12°C        | Dense fog significantly reduces visibility |
| 19    | Windy         | 17°C        | Strong winds but clear skies               |
| 20    | Light Snow    | -2°C        | Gentle snowfall in cold conditions         |

**Usage:** Ideal for day-to-day weather in most campaign settings. Provides realistic variety without disrupting gameplay.

| 2    | Gravity Flux           | Areas of altered gravity cause chaos             |
| 3    | Magic Storm            | Wild magic surges with electrical phenomena      |

**Usage:** Perfect for:

- High-magic campaign settings
- Areas near ley lines or magical nexus points
- Planar boundaries or dimensional rifts
- Special events or story moments
- Use in **Hybrid Mode** for occasional magical weather

## Hybrid Mode

When using **Hybrid Mode**, the module randomly chooses between your normal and strange weather tables based on the configured probability. This creates an unpredictable mix perfect for:

- Campaigns transitioning from low to high magic
- Areas where magic is becoming more prevalent
- Regions near magical phenomena
- Adding occasional surprises to standard weather

**Recommended Settings:**

- **Low Magic**: 5-15% strange weather chance
- **Medium Magic**: 20-35% strange weather chance
- **High Magic**: 40-60% strange weather chance

## Customization

All weather patterns include detailed attribute data:

- **Temperature** (Celsius, auto-converts to player preference)
- **Cloud Cover** (0-100%)
- **Precipitation** (0-100%)
- **Wind Speed** (km/h)
- **Visibility** (0-100%)
- **Intensity** (overall severity, 0-100)

You can duplicate and modify these tables, or create your own! See [EXAMPLE-ROLLTABLE.md](EXAMPLE-ROLLTABLE.md) for details on creating custom weather tables.
